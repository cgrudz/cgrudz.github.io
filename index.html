Short biography

I was awarded my PhD in Applied Mathematics from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in May 2016. I am now a postdoctoral researcher at the Nansen Environmental and Remote Sensing Center. During Fall Semester 2018 I will be a visitor at CEREA, a Joint Laboratory of École des Ponts ParisTech/EDF R&D. Beginning Spring Semester 2019, I will join the University of Nevada, Reno as an assistant professor of statistics.

I spent most of my years in Eugene, Oregon before moving around the world. I am a soul, jazz and post-punk devotee and DIY motorcycle and Linux enthusiast. I like reading and re-reading Foucault and Zhuang Zhou.
