Problem 1:

Let the matrix \(\mathbf{X} \in \mathbb{R}^{n\times p}\), for \(p < n\). We assume that the columns of \(\mathbf{X}\) are linearly independent such that \(\left(\mathbf{X}^\mathrm{T}\mathbf{X}\right)^{-1}\) exists. Define \(\mathbf{H} \triangleq \mathbf{X} \left(\mathbf{X}^\mathrm{T}\mathbf{X}\right)^{-1} \mathbf{X}^\mathrm{T}\). Prove that \(\mathbf{H}^2 = \mathbf{H}\).
