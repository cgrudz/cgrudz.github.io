Problem 2:

Suppose that for some \(\boldsymbol{\beta}\in\mathbb{R}^2\) and \(\boldsymbol{\epsilon} \sim N\left(0, \boldsymbol{\Sigma}\right)\), the following equation holds

\[\begin{align} \mathbf{Y} = \mathbf{X} \boldsymbol{\beta} + \boldsymbol{\epsilon}. \end{align}\]

Suppose that \(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}\) is of the form, \[\begin{align} \boldsymbol{\Sigma} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 10 \end{pmatrix}. \end{align}\]

Does the first or second observation receive more weight in weighted least squares? Why?
